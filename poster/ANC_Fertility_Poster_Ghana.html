1. Background & Abstract

Spatial analysis of antenatal care (ANC) access inequities across Ghana's 261 districts using 2022 DHS and 2021 Census data. Bivariate LISA identifies co-clustering of low ANC coverage with high fertility. Random Forest and XGBoost with SHAP pinpoint poverty, female illiteracy, and facility distance as dominant predictors. Getis-Ord Gi* confirms 31 High-High hotspot districts in Northern and Savannah regions.

2. Data Sources

Primary: 2022 Ghana Demographic and Health Survey (GSS · GHS · ICF International).

Covariate: 2021 Ghana Population and Housing Census — 261 district-level predictors.

Spatial: Ghana 261-district boundary GeoJSON (Ghana Statistical Service, 2021).

3. Ethical Statement

All data are publicly available, de-identified secondary datasets. No individual-level identifiers were accessed. Analysis used ecological district-level aggregates only.

8. Discussion & Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates strong spatial patterning in anc fertility inequities across Ghana's 261 districts. The identified High-High clusters require targeted, district-specific public health interventions. ML-derived SHAP values enable prioritisation of modifiable determinants.
